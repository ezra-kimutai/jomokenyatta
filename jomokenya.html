Mzee Jomo Kenyatta

The man who led Kenya to Independence

Here's a time line of Mzee Jomo Kenyatta's life:

  • 1897 - Born in Nginda, Kenya
  • 1907 - Suffered jigger infection.
  • 1912 - Completed his mission school education.
  • 1914 - Baptized at the church of Scotland Mission.Changed his name from John Peter Kamau to John Kamau.
  • 1919 - Marries his first wife, Grace Wahu.
  • 1920 - His first son, Peter Muigai, was born.
  • 1922 - Adopted the name Jomo, a Kikuyu name, meaning 'Burning spear'. Joined a political party called EAA(East African Association)
  • 1928 - Launched a monthly Kikuyu paper called Mwigwithania
  • 1929 - Dispatched to London to represent the KCA
  • 1951 - Took part in Mau Mau rebellion
  • 1959 - Released from prison
    • 1963 - Led Kenya to Independence, becoming Kenya's first president.
  • 1978 - Died

"President Kenyatta's resilience alongside other freedom fighters led Kenya to Independence"
